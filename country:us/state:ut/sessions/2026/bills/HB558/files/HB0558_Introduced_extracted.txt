1920A-9-203.50Elections Amendments2026 GENERAL SESSIONSTATE OF UTAHChief Sponsor: Ryan D. WilcoxSenate Sponsor: LONG TITLEGeneral Description:This bill requires the posting of certain information relating to a candidate for public office.Highlighted Provisions:This bill:requires an election officer to post the following information relating to a candidate for public office:the name and campaign contact information of the candidate;the office that the candidate is seeking; andthe political party affiliation of the candidate, regardless of whether the candidate is a candidate for partisan or nonpartisan office or seeking retention election. Money Appropriated in this Bill:NoneOther Special Clauses:NoneUtah Code Sections Affected:AMENDS:20A-9-203.5, as enacted by Laws of Utah 2016, Chapter 48Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:Section 1. Section 20A-9-203.5 is amended to read:20A-9-203.5. Requirement to post candidate information on website.The clerk of a county or a municipality(1)Except as provided in Subsection (2), an election officer shall, within three business days after the day on which the clerkelection officer accepts a declaration of candidacy, post the following information on the election officer's website of the county or municipality:(1)(a)the name and campaign contact information of the candidate; and(2)(b)the office that the candidate is seeking.; and(c)regardless of whether the office sought by the candidate is a partisan or nonpartisan office, and regardless of whether the election is a retention election:(i)the political party with which the candidate is affiliated; or(ii)if the candidate is not affiliated with a political party, that the candidate is not affiliated with a political party.(2)For an election officer who does not have a website, the election officer shall, within three business days after the day on which the election officer accepts a declaration of candidacy, publish the information described in Subsection (1) on the Utah Public Notice Website.Section 2.  Effective Date.This bill takes effect on May 6, 2026.2-13-26 9:19 AM
