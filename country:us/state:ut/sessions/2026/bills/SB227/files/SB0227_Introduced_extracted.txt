678B-8-2040Punitive Damages Amendments2026 GENERAL SESSIONSTATE OF UTAHChief Sponsor: Ronald M. WintertonHouse Sponsor: LONG TITLEGeneral Description:This bill enacts provisions relating to limitations on liability for punitive damages.Highlighted Provisions:This bill:defines "motor vehicle";provides a limit on an award of punitive damages resulting from a personal injury claim that involves a motor vehicle; andprovides a limit on the attorney fees related to an award of punitive damages.Money Appropriated in this Bill:NoneOther Special Clauses:NoneUtah Code Sections Affected:ENACTS:78B-8-204, Utah Code Annotated 1953Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:Section 1. Section 78B-8-204 is enacted to read:78B-8-204. Limitations on award of punitive damages for a personal injury claim involving a motor vehicle.(1)As used in this section, "motor vehicle" means the same as that term is defined in Section 41-1a-102.(2)If the basis for punitive damages under Section 78B-8-201 is based on the tortfeasor's operation of a motor vehicle and the cause of action arises out of a personal injury claim, an award of punitive damages may not exceed an amount equal to the greater of:(a)the sum of:(i)two times the amount of economic damages; and(ii)an amount equal to any noneconomic damages not exceeding $750,000; or(b)$200,000.(3)If a court awards a person punitive damages in accordance with this section, attorney fees for the person may not exceed 25% of the amount of the total award of punitive damages.Section 2.  Effective Date.This bill takes effect on May 6, 2026.1-30-26 9:52 AM
