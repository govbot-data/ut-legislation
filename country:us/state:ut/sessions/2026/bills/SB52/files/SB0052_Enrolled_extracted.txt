353E-6-9010Substitute Teaching Requirements Amendments2026 GENERAL SESSIONSTATE OF UTAHChief Sponsor: Lincoln FillmoreHouse Sponsor: Tracy J. MillerLONG TITLEGeneral Description:This bill removes the requirement for a teaching license for a long-term substitute.Highlighted Provisions:This bill:removes the requirement for a teaching license for a long-term substitute.Money Appropriated in this Bill:NoneOther Special Clauses:NoneUtah Code Sections Affected:AMENDS:53E-6-901, as last amended by Laws of Utah 2024, Chapter 22Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:Section 1. Section 53E-6-901 is amended to read:53E-6-901. Substitute teachers.(1)As used in this section, "substitute teacher" means a licensed or non-licensed individual who is employed by a school district to fill in for a regular classroom teacher during the teacher's temporary absence from the classroom.(2)When hiring substitute teachers, school districts shall prioritize licensed educators as substitutes when available.(3)An individual shall submit to a background check in accordance with Section 53G-11-402 prior to employment as a substitute teacher.(4)A teacher's position in the classroom may not be filled by a non-licensed substitute teacher for more than a total of 20 days during any school year unless a licensed educator is not available.(5)(4)An individual who is ineligible to hold a license for reasons described in Title 53E, Chapter 6, Part 6, License Denial and Discipline, may not serve as a substitute teacher.Section 2.  Effective Date.This bill takes effect on May 6, 2026.12-29-25 12:23 PM
