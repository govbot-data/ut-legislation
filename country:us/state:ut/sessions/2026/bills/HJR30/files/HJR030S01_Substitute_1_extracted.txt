20House Joint Resolution to Approve the Transfer of Land2026 GENERAL SESSIONSTATE OF UTAHChief Sponsor: Casey SniderSenate Sponsor: Scott D. SandallLONG TITLEGeneral Description:This resolution approves the proposed transfer of state-owned lands. Highlighted Provisions:This resolution:acknowledges that the state and the United States government have disputed the ownership of 3,760 acres of land along the Great Salt Lake;describes the terms of a proposed settlement agreement between the state and the United States government for the transfer of 22,311 acres of land located below the ordinary high water mark of the Great Salt Lake (settlement lands);describes the process for a state agency to obtain approval from the Legislature for the transfer of 500 or more acres of state-owned lands to the United States government; andapproves the transfer of the settlement lands to the United States government as part of the proposed settlement negotiated between the state and the United States government.Money Appropriated in this Bill:NoneOther Special Clauses:NoneBe it resolved by the Legislature of the state of Utah:WHEREAS, the state of Utah and the United States government have disputed, among other issues, the ownership of 3,760 acres of land (disputed lands) used for wildlife refuge along the Great Salt Lake, in a pending lawsuit, State of Utah v. United States, No. 2:12-cv-00378-HCN-DAO (D. Utah);WHEREAS, the state of Utah through the Division of Forestry, Fire and State Lands, created in Utah Code Section 65A-1-4, has negotiated a proposed settlement agreement with the United States government that transfers title of 22,311 acres of land located below the ordinary high water mark of the Great Salt Lake (settlement lands) to the United States government and resolves all remaining disputes, including any title disputes, related to the disputed lands;WHEREAS, the proposed settlement provides for the transfer of the settlement lands to the United States government for the payment of at least $60,000,000 to the state of Utah, which shall be used exclusively for the benefit of the Great Salt Lake;WHEREAS, the proposed settlement provides that the United States government, through the United States Fish and Wildlife Service, shall provide public use and access to the settlement lands consistent with the National Wildlife Refuge System Administration Act, 16 U.S.C. Secs. 668dd through 668ee, and other federal statutes, rules, and regulations, as applicable;WHEREAS, the proposed settlement provides that, if the United States government disposes of settlement lands in the future, the United States government shall offer the state of Utah a first right to purchase the settlement lands or obtain the settlement lands in a land exchange;WHEREAS, the proposed settlement provides that the state of Utah reserves and retains all mineral interests, including subsurface and suspended minerals within the waters or brines of the Great Salt Lake;WHEREAS, in accordance with Utah Code Subsection 63L-2-201(2), a state agency shall submit to the Legislature, for the Legislature's approval or rejection, a proposed agreement to sell or transfer to the United States government 500 or more acres of state controlled lands; andWHEREAS, the United States government, principally through the United States Department of Justice and the United States Department of the Interior, including the United States Fish and Wildlife Service, will conduct a settlement review and approval process before making a final determination about the proposed settlement agreement:NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of the state of Utah approves the proposed agreement negotiated by the Division of Forestry, Fire and State Lands with the United States government for the transfer of the settlement lands.3-6-26 1:12 PM
