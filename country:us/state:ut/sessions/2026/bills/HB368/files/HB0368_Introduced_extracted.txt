1426B-7-1240Cold Plunge Amendments2026 GENERAL SESSIONSTATE OF UTAHChief Sponsor: David ShallenbergerSenate Sponsor: LONG TITLEGeneral Description:This bill amends provisions related to public cold baths.Highlighted Provisions:This bill:exempts from rules made by the Department of Health and Human Services (department) public cold baths in an apartment building;amends exemptions from department rules for other public cold baths, including in apartment buildings;defines terms; andmakes technical and conforming changes.Money Appropriated in this Bill:NoneOther Special Clauses:NoneUtah Code Sections Affected:AMENDS:26B-7-124, as enacted by Laws of Utah 2024, Chapter 478Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:Section 1. Section 26B-7-124 is amended to read:26B-7-124. Rules for public cold baths.(1)As used in this section,:(a)"Common areas" means the non-dwelling communal areas accessible to all residents of an apartment building.(b) "public"Public cold bath" means a tub or tank that:(a)(i)is used by, regardless of whether there is a charge or payment for use:(i)(A)(I)the general public, regardless of whether there is a charge or payment for use; and; or(II)residents of an apartment building in the common areas; and(ii)(B)one batherup to five bathers at a time;(b)(ii)contains chilled water that is:(i)(A)maintained at a temperature lower than 60 degrees Fahrenheit;(ii)(B)no more than 180650 gallons in volume; and(iii)(C)at a depth that allows the bather to maintain the bather's head above the water while in a seated position; and(c)(iii)continuously filters and sanitizes the chilled water.(2)The department may not adopt a rule that restricts, limits, or imposes requirements on the operation of a public cold bath.Section 2.  Effective Date.This bill takes effect on May 6, 2026.1-26-26 9:37 AM
