159 Section 2. Section 2 is enacted to read:
160 63M-7-215.1. Violent Crime Clearance Rate Fund -- Funding -- Uses.
160 (1) As used in this section:
161 (a) "Cleared by arrest" means that an individual reported in connection with a crime is:
162 (i) arrested by a law enforcement agency;
163 (ii) charged with a crime; and
164 (iii) turned over to a court for prosecution for the crime.
165 (b) "Cleared by exceptional means" means that a law enforcement agency:
166 (i) has identified an individual reported in connection with a crime;
167 (ii) has sufficient evidence to arrest the individual;
168 (iii) knows the individual's exact location; and
169 (iv) has encountered factors beyond the law enforcement agency's control that prevent the arrest,
charging, and prosecution of the individual.
171 (c) "Fund" means the Violent Crime Clearance Rate Fund created in this section.
172 (d) "Law enforcement agency" means the same as that term is defined in Section 53-1-102.
174 (e) "Rural community" means the same as that term is defined in Section 63N-4-801.
175 (f) "Violent crime" means a violent felony, as that term is defined in Section 76-3-203.5.
176 (g) "Violent crime clearance rate" means the percentage obtained by dividing the total number of
violent crimes cleared by arrest and cleared by exceptional means by the total number of violent
crimes reported.
179 (2) There is created a special revenue fund known as the "Violent Crime Clearance Rate Fund."
