andcertify the number of valid signatures to the lieutenant governor;  for an elective office described above, requires the lieutenant governor to:count the total number of valid signatures received from each county clerk; andif the aggregate number of valid signatures is sufficient for the candidate to qualify for placement on the ballot, certify the signatures;for a candidate for elective office seeking the nomination of a qualified political party through signature-gathering, moves the deadline for the candidate to submit signatures to an election official from 14 to 21 days before the day on which the party holds a convention to nominate a candidate for the same elective office;
