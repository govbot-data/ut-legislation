Section 26B-4-516 is enacted to read:26B-4-516. Voluntary participation.Sections 26B-4-517 through 26B-4-519 do not create a duty or standard of care for a person to prescribe or dispense ivermectin.Section 3. Section 26B-4-517 is enacted to read:26B-4-517. Authorization to dispense ivermectin.Notwithstanding Title 58, Chapter 17b, Pharmacy Practice Act, a person licensed under Title 58, Chapter 17b, Pharmacy Practice Act, to dispense ivermectin may dispense ivermectin:(1)to a patient who is 18 years old or older;(2)pursuant to a standing prescription drug order issued in accordance with Section 26B-4-518;(3)without any other prescription drug order from a person licensed to prescribe ivermectin; and(4)in accordance with the dispensing guidelines in Section 26B-4-519.Section 4. Section 26B-4-518 is enacted to read:26B-4-518. Standing prescription drug orders for ivermectin.A physician who is licensed to prescribe ivermectin, including a physician acting in the physician's capacity as an employee of the department, or a medical director of a local health department, may issue a standing prescription drug order authorizing the dispensing of ivermectin under Section 26B-4-517 in accordance with protocol that:(1)requires the physician to specify the persons, by professional license number, authorized to dispense ivermectin;(2)requires the physician to review at least annually the dispensing practices of those authorized by the physician to dispense ivermectin; and(3)is approved by the Division of Professional Licensing within the Department of Commerce by administrative rule made in accordance with Title 63G, Chapter 3, Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act.Section 5. Section 26B-4-519 is enacted to read:26B-4-519. Guidelines for dispensing ivermectin.(1)As used in this section:(a)"Board of Pharmacy" means the Utah State Board of Pharmacy created in Section 58-17b-201.(b)"Medical Licensing Board" means the Medical Licensing Board created in Section 58-67-201.(2)Before a pharmacist or pharmacy intern dispenses ivermectin under Section 26B-4-517, the pharmacist or pharmacy intern:(a)shall obtain from the patient a completed screening risk assessment questionnaire described in Subsection (4)(a); and(b)if the results of the screening risk assessment questionnaire described in Subsection (2)(a) indicate that it is unsafe to dispense ivermectin to a patient, may not dispense the ivermectin to the patient.(3)A pharmacist or pharmacy intern who dispenses ivermectin under Section 26B-4-517, shall provide the patient with the standardized fact sheet described in Subsection (4)(b).(4)The division, in collaboration with the Board of Pharmacy and the Medical Licensing Board, shall make rules in accordance with Title 63G, Chapter 3, Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act, to establish:(a)a screening risk assessment questionnaire to determine a patient's suitability for treatment with ivermectin that:(i)identifies contraindications;(ii)assesses risk factors;(iii)ensures the informed consent of the patient to whom ivermectin is dispensed; and(iv)assists a pharmacist or pharmacy intern in determining whether dispensing ivermectin to the patient is appropriate; and(b)a standardized fact sheet that includes:(i)the indications and contraindications for the use of ivermectin;(ii)the appropriate administration of ivermectin;(iii)when the patient should see a health care provider for follow-up care or seek emergency medical care; and(iv)any other information the division, the Board of Pharmacy, and the Medical Licensing Board deem appropriate.Section 6. Section 26B-4-520 is enacted to read:26B-4-520. Limited civil liability for standing prescription drug orders for ivermectin.A physician who issues a standing prescription drug order in accordance with Section 26B-4-518 is not liable for any civil damages for acts or omissions resulting from the dispensing of ivermectin under Sections 26B-4-517 and 26B-4-519.Section 7. Section 58-17b-508 is enacted to read:58-17b-508. Ivermectin -- Immunity from liability -- Exclusion from unlawful or unprofessional conduct.(1)As used in this section, "ivermectin" means the same as that term is defined in Section 26B-4-501.(2)A person licensed under this chapter that dispenses ivermectin pursuant to a standing prescription drug order issued in accordance with Section 26B-4-518, is not liable for any civil damages for actions authorized by Sections 26B-4-517 and 26B-4-519, except for damages resulting from gross negligence or willful misconduct.(3)It is not unprofessional conduct or unlawful conduct for a licensee under this chapter to dispense ivermectin pursuant to a standing prescription drug order issued in accordance with Section 26B-4-518.Section 8.
