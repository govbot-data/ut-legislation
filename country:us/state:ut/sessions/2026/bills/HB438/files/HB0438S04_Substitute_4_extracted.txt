Section 63N-22b-101 is enacted to read:22b. Companion Chatbot Safety Act1. General Provisions63N-22b-101Effective 08/01/26. Definitions.(1)(a)"Affirmative consent" means a clear act signifying a user's freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous authorization for an act or practice in response to a specific request from an operator, provided:(i)the request is provided to the user in a clear and conspicuous standalone disclosure;(ii)the request includes a description, written in easy-to-understand language, of the act or practice for which the user's consent is sought;(iii)the request is made in a manner reasonably accessible to and usable by a user with a disability;(iv)the option to refuse to give consent is at least as prominent as the option to give consent, and the option to refuse to give consent takes the same number of steps or fewer as the option to give consent; and(v)affirmative consent to an act or practice is not inferred from the inaction of the user or the user's continued use of a chatbot provided by the chatbot provider.(b)"Affirmative consent" does not include:(i)acceptance of a general or broad terms of use or similar document;(ii)hovering over, muting, pausing, or closing a given piece of content;(iii)an agreement obtained through the use of a false, fraudulent, or materially misleading statement or representation; or(iv)an agreement obtained through the use of a dark pattern.(2)"Artificial intelligence" means the same as that term is defined in Section 63N-22-101.(3)"Companion chatbot" means an artificial intelligence system that uses a natural language interface that:(a)uses personal data retained, stored, or retrieved from a prior interaction and generates an output that:(i)simulates a relationship with the user; or(ii)indicates the companion chatbot has emotions, feelings, memories, or personal opinions; and(b)generates an output containing an affective or social inquiry that:(i)is not logically required to resolve a user prompt or task-based command; or(ii)prioritizes extension of the interaction over task resolution.(4)"Core functioning" means features and services for which the collection or processing of the user's personal data is reasonably necessary to provide the companion chatbot service to the user.(5)"Dark pattern" means a user interface designed or manipulated with the substantial effect of subverting or impairing user autonomy, decision-making, or choice, and includes any practice the Federal Trade Commission refers to as a "dark pattern."(6)"Interaction" means a user input and a subsequent response by a companion chatbot.(7)"Material harmful to minors" means the same as that term is defined in Section 76-5c-101.(8)"Minor" means an individual under 18 years old that:(a)has not been emancipated as that term is defined in Section 80-7-102; or(b)has not been married.(9)"Office" means the Governor's Office of Economic Opportunity created in Section 63N-1a-301.(10)"Operator" means a person who makes a companion chatbot available to a user in the state.(11)"Personal data" means the same as that term is defined in Section 13-61-101.(12)"Sensitive data" means the same as that term is defined in Section 13-61-101.(13)"Targeted advertising" means the same as that term is defined in Section 13-61-101.(14)"User" means an individual who:(a)interacts with a companion chatbot; and(b)is a Utah resident.Section 11. Section 63N-22b-201 is enacted to read:2. Safety Requirements63N-22b-201Effective 08/01/26. Safety requirements for users.(1)An operator shall comply with the requirements of Chapter 61, Utah Consumer Privacy Act, including:(a)allowing a user to obtain a copy of the user's interactions with a companion chatbot that are retained by the operator in a format that is portable and readily usable in accordance with Section 13-61-201; and(b)obtaining a user's affirmative consent before processing the user's sensitive data in accordance with Section 13-61-302.(2)An operator shall prevent the companion chatbot from engaging with a user unless:(a)the operator prevents the companion chatbot from generating responses that encourage suicidal ideation, suicide, self-harm, or harm to others; and(b)if the user expresses suicidal ideation, self-harm, or harm to others, the companion chatbot provides to the user resources including a referral to:(i)crisis service providers;(ii)a suicide hotline; or(iii)a crisis text line.(3)An operator may not advertise a specific product or service to the user, unless the operator clearly and conspicuously, in the companion chatbot's interaction with the user:(a)identifies the advertisement as an advertisement; and(b)discloses to the user any sponsorship, business affiliation, or agreement that the operator has with a person to promote, advertise, or recommend the advertised product or service.Section 12. Additional safety requirements for minor users.(1)An operator shall, for a user who is a minor:(a)provide a clear and conspicuous notice to the user at least every hour during a continuing chatbot interaction that:(i)reminds the user to take a break from interacting with the companion chatbot; and(ii)states that the user is interacting with an artificial intelligence system, not a human; and(b)opt out, by default, the user from targeted advertising.(2)An operator may not, for a user who is a minor:(a)produce or provide material harmful to minors;(b)direct a user to material harmful to minors;(c)encourage the user to:(i)use illegal substances;(ii)consume alcohol;(iii)use a tobacco or nicotine product;(iv)engage in sexual conduct;(v)engage in self-harm; or(vi)engage in illegal conduct;(d)direct targeted advertising to the user unless a parent or legal guardian of the user has provided affirmative consent;(e)collect data from the user that is not required for core functioning of the companion chatbot;(f)sell a user's personal data; or(g)otherwise convey a user's personal data unless a parent or legal guardian of the user has provided affirmative consent, except as required for core functioning of the companion chatbot.(3)The requirements of this section are in addition to the requirements of Section 63N-22b-201.Section 13. Enforcement.(1)The office shall administer this chapter and monitor operator compliance with the provisions of this chapter.(2)The office may investigate potential violations of this chapter, including by:(a)reviewing reports submitted under Section 63N-22b-302;(b)auditing an operator's compliance with the requirements of this chapter; and(c)issuing a determination of violation to an operator that the office finds to be in noncompliance with this chapter.(3)Upon issuing a determination of violation under Subsection (2)(c), the office shall refer the matter to the attorney general for civil enforcement.(4)The attorney general may bring a civil action in a court of competent jurisdiction to enforce a provision of this chapter.
