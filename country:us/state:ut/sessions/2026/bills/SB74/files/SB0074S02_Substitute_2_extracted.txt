MusselmanHouse Sponsor: LONG TITLEGeneral Description:This bill enacts requirements for a time-limited demand letter.Highlighted Provisions:This bill:enacts requirements for a time-limited demand letter related to a motor vehicle insurance claim;provides parameters and timing for a time-limited demand letter; andaddresses an insurer's duties and an insured's rights and remedies in relation to a time-limited demand letter.Money Appropriated in this Bill:NoneOther Special Clauses:NoneUtah Code Sections Affected:ENACTS:31A-22-323, Utah Code Annotated 1953Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:Section 1. Section 31A-22-323 is enacted to read:31A-22-323. Time-limited demand letter -- Requirements.(1)A person issuing a time-limited demand to settle any claim under this part shall ensure that the letter:(a)is in writing;(b)is labeled as a time-limited demand to settle;(c)references this section; and(d)contains material terms, including:(i)the time period within which the demand is required to be accepted, which shall be no fewer than 45 days from the date on which the letter is received by certified mail;(ii)a clear and unequivocal offer to settle all claims within policy limits, including the satisfaction of all known liens;(iii)an offer for a complete release from the claimant of all present and future claims arising from the occurrence against the insured;(iv)the date and location of the loss;(v)the claim number, if known;(vi)a description of all known injuries alleged to have been sustained by the claimant;(vii)reasonable proof of injury sufficient to demonstrate that the claimant sustained an actual injury arising from the occurrence, including:(A)medical records;(B)billing statements; and(C)sworn statements or other documentation supporting the existence of the injury; and(viii)if the demand references or suggests the possibility of placing a judicial lien against personal property, a plain-language explanation of the process for obtaining a judicial lien and a statement advising the insured of the insured's right to consult independent legal counsel.(2)The reasonable proof of injury described in Subsection (1)(a)(vii) does not require proof, valuation, or quantification of non-economic damages, including pain and suffering.(3)If the insurer or insured reasonably determines that a demand letter fails to include required material information, the insurer or insured may provide written notice identifying the deficiency, and the response period is tolled until the deficiency is cured.(4)(a)Within a reasonable time after receipt of a time-limited demand letter that complies with this section, the liability insurer shall provide the insured with a written disclosure stating:(i)whether the insurer agrees to defend the insured against the claim; and(ii)whether the insurer agrees to indemnify the insured for any judgment or settlement arising from the claim, including whether such indemnification is limited to the applicable policy limits.(b)A disclosure made under this Subsection (4):(i)does not expand, reduce, or modify coverage under the insurance policy; and(ii)is intended solely to provide clarity to the insured regarding the insurer's position.(5)(a)A time-limited demand to settle a claim under this section may not include demands for economic damages in amounts that exceed the economic damages reasonably supported by the documentation provided with the demand.(b)Nothing in this subsection requires documentation or valuation of non-economic damages.(6)Nothing in this section may be construed to alter, limit, or waive:(a)an insurer's duty to act in good faith and deal fairly with the insurer's insured; or(b)any rights or remedies available to an insured arising from an insurer's failure to accept a reasonable settlement offer within applicable policy limits.Section 2.
