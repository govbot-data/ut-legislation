MusselmanHouse Sponsor: LONG TITLEGeneral Description:This bill amends seat belt laws to allow the misuse of or failure to use a seat belt to be considered for contributory or comparative negligence in civil litigation.Highlighted Provisions:This bill:amends the Traffic Code to allow the misuse of or failure to use a seat belt to be considered as contributory or comparative negligence in civil litigation.Money Appropriated in this Bill:NoneOther Special Clauses:NoneUtah Code Sections Affected:AMENDS:41-6a-1806, as renumbered and amended by Laws of Utah 2005, Chapter 2Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:Section 1. Section 41-6a-1806 is amended to read:41-6a-1806. Compliance -- Civil litigation.The misuse or failure to use a child restraint device or misuse or failure to wear a safety belt:(1)does not may constitute contributory or comparative negligence on the part of a person seeking recovery for injuries; and(2)may not be introduced as evidence in any civil litigation on the issue of negligence, injuries, or the mitigation of damages.Section 2.  Effective Date.This bill takes effect on May 6, 2026.1-5-26 11:52 AM
