460 (i) the division, or another agency responsible for placement, files a written petition with the juvenile
court requesting the extension and setting forth good cause; and
462 (ii) the juvenile court enters a written finding that it is in the best interests of both the minor and the
community to extend the period of detention.
464 (d) The juvenile court may extend the period of detention beyond the seven calendar days if the juvenile
court finds, by clear and convincing evidence, that:
466 (i) the division, or another agency responsible for placement, does not have space for the minor; and
468 (ii) the safety of the minor and community requires an extension of the period of detention.
470 (e) The division, or the agency with custody of the minor, shall report to the juvenile court every
48 hours, excluding weekends and holidays, regarding whether the division, or another agency
responsible for placement, has space for the minor.
473 (f) The division, or agency, requesting an extension shall promptly notify the detention facility that a
written petition has been filed.
475 (g) The juvenile court shall promptly notify the detention facility regarding the juvenile court's initial
disposition and any ruling on a petition for an extension, whether granted or denied.
