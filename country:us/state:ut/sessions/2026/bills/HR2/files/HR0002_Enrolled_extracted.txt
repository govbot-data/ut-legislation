BallardLONG TITLEGeneral Description:This rules resolution modifies the duties of a House standing committee.Highlighted Provisions:This resolution:defines terms;when considering legislation that proposes to make an occupation a regulated occupation, requires a House standing committee to determine whether the Office of Professional Licensure Review conducted a sunrise review of the occupation; andmakes technical and conforming changes.Money Appropriated in this Bill:NoneOther Special Clauses:NoneLegislative Rules Affected:AMENDS:HR3-2-402Be it resolved by the House of Representatives of the state of Utah:Section 1. HR3-2-402 is amended to read:HR3-2-402. Standing committee consideration of legislation -- Regulated occupation -- Fiscal impact.(1)As used in this rule:(a)"Office" means the Office of Professional Licensure Review created in Utah Code Title 13, Chapter 1b, Office of Professional Licensure Review.(b)"Regulated occupation" means the same as that term is defined in Utah Code Section 13-1b-101.(2)When considering legislation that proposes to make an occupation a regulated occupation, a standing committee shall determine whether the office conducted a sunrise review of the occupation in accordance with Utah Code Title 13, Chapter 1b, Part 3, Office Review and Reporting.(3)(a)(a)(i)A standing committee may not reviewconsider legislation unless the legislation has an approved fiscal note.(b)(ii)Notwithstanding Subsection (1)(a)(3)(a)(i), a standing committee may consider a substitute not previously adopted, regardless of whether the substitute has an approved fiscal note.(2)(b)Except as provided in HR3-2-401, a standing committee in one or both chambers shall reviewconsider legislation before the legislation is held in the opposite chamber because of the legislation's fiscal impact.Section 2.
