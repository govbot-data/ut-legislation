23 26B-4-327. Street medicine.
26 (1) As used in this section:
27 (a) "Street medicine" means health care that is provided by a licensed health care provider, who
conducts patient visits outside of a health care facility, clinic, or shelter to an individual
experiencing unsheltered homelessness.
30 (b)
(i) "Unsheltered homelessness" means not having a long-term or permanent structure in which to live.
32 (ii) "Unsheltered homelessness" may include sleeping in a vehicle, abandoned building, farm,
wilderness, street, park, or encampment.
34 (2) A person providing street medicine shall not engage in conduct that requires a license under Title
58, Occupations and Professions, unless the person holds the applicable license.
37 {(3) }
{(a) {The University of Utah Health, in consultation with existing street medicine providers, shall
study the feasibility of establishing a street medicine program to serve Davis, Salt Lake, and Utah
counties.} }
40 {(b) {The University of Utah Health shall provide a written report with findings and recommendations
to the Health and Human Services Interim Committee on or before August 31, 2027.} }
35 (3) A street medicine provider may dispense and administer a long-acting injectable in accordance with
Section 58-88-202.
43 (4) In accordance with Title 63G, Chapter 3, Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act, the department may
make rules permitting the department and street medicine providers to share and receive certain
health information with the Utah Homeless Management Information System, described in Section
35A-16-202.
