66 Section 2. Section 2 is enacted to read:
67
Part 3. Limitations on Use of Public Funds
68 63G-29-301. Public funding for transgender treatment or procedure prohibited -- Exception
-- Coverage for detransitioning.
70 (1) A governmental entity may not expend public funds to pay or otherwise reimburse, either directly or
indirectly, any person for the performance of:
72 (a) a primary sex characteristic surgical procedure as defined in Section 58-67-102; or
73 (b) a secondary sex change characteristic surgical procedure as defined in Section 58-67-102.
75 (2) A government entity that, before May 6, 2026, via insurance coverage, paid or otherwise reimbursed
a person, either directly or indirectly, for the treatment or a surgical procedure described in
Subsection (1) that was performed before May 6, 2026, to transition an individual from the
individual's biological sex, the government entity shall offer the same type and amount of insurance
coverage for treatment or a surgical procedure to restore that individual, to the extent possible, to
that individual's biological sex.
