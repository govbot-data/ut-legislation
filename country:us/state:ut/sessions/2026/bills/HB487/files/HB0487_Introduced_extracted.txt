336-3-3070Legislator Autonomy Amendments2026 GENERAL SESSIONSTATE OF UTAHChief Sponsor: Matt MacPhersonSenate Sponsor: LONG TITLEGeneral Description:This bill enacts provisions related to legislator activities.Highlighted Provisions:This bill:provides that a legislator may introduce and vote on legislation according to the legislator's own judgment and conscience.Money Appropriated in this Bill:NoneOther Special Clauses:NoneUtah Code Sections Affected:ENACTS:36-3-307, Utah Code Annotated 1953Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:Section 1. Section 36-3-307 is enacted to read:36-3-307. Legislator autonomy.(1)Each sitting legislator is free to perform the following legislative activities according to the legislator's own judgment and conscience:(a)introducing or refraining from introducing any legislation, including legislation that enacts, repeals, or amends a provision of the Utah Code or uncodified law; and(b)voting for or against, or refraining from voting on, any legislation.(2)The provisions of this section are subject to legislative rules.Section 2.  Effective Date.This bill takes effect on May 6, 2026.2-4-26 10:28 AM
