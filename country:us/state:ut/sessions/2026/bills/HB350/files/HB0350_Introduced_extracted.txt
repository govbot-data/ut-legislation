753G-9-205.20Foods Available at Schools Amendments2026 GENERAL SESSIONSTATE OF UTAHChief Sponsor: Kristen ChevrierSenate Sponsor: Heidi BalderreeLONG TITLEGeneral Description:This bill amends provisions related to food additive prohibitions in a public school.Highlighted Provisions:This bill:amends the list of prohibited food additives;adds an exception related to food that is donated;removes an exception related to small schools; andmakes technical and conforming changes.Money Appropriated in this Bill:NoneOther Special Clauses:This bill provides a special effective date.Utah Code Sections Affected:AMENDS:53G-9-205.2, as enacted by Laws of Utah 2025, Chapter 466Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:Section 1. Section 53G-9-205.2 is amended to read:53G-9-205.2. Definitions -- Prohibited food additives -- Exceptions.(1)As used in this section:(a)"FDC" means the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetics Act, 21 C.F.R. Part 74.(b)"Public school" means the same as that term is defined in Section 53G-9-205.1.(c)"School day" means the same as that terms is defined in Section 53G-6-201.(2)(a)Beginning in the 2026-2027 school year, a A public school may not sell, donate, offer, or serve on school grounds during the school day, food that contains:(i)potassium bromate;(ii)propylparaben;(iii)titanium dioxide;(iii)(iv)FDC Blue No. 1;(iv)(v)FDC Blue No. 2;(v)(vi)FDC Green No. 3;(vi)(vii)FDC Red No. 3;(vii)(viii)FDC Red No. 40;(viii)(ix)FDC Yellow No. 5; and(ix)(x)FDC Yellow No. 6.(b)Except as provided in Subsection (3), a person who sells, donates, offers, or serves a consumable item during the school day shall comply with the requirements under this section.(3)(a)Notwithstanding Subsection (2)(b):(i)(a)a student's parent may provide students with a food item described in Subsection (2)(a) during the school day;(ii)(b)a teacher may provide students with a food item described in Subsection (2)(a) if the teacher obtains permission from the students' parents at the start of each school semester; and(iii)(c)an individual may donate a food item described in Subsection (2)(a) to a school's food pantry;(d)a public school may accept and distribute to a student a food item:(i)described in Subsection (2)(a); and(ii)donated under Subsection (3)(c); and(e)a person may sell a food item described in Subsection (2)(a):(A)(i)as a concession item at a school-sponsored event or extra-curricular activity; or(B)(ii)in a vending machine.(b)This section does not apply to a charter school or school district that has 5,500 students or fewer.Section 2.  Effective Date.This bill takes effect on July 1, 2026.1-23-26 9:52 AM
