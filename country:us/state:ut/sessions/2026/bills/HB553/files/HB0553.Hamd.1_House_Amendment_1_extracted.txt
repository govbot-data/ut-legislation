H.B. 553
Dog Attack Amendments
House Amendments Amendment 1 March 02, 2026 10:57 AM
Representative Jason E. Thompson proposes the following amendments:
1. Line 7 through 12:
7 This Bill:
8 →establishes that an individual injured in a dog attack may bring a claim for damages
9 within four years of the injury;
→provides that a dog owner is not liable for injury or death caused by the owner's dog
if the dog is a livestock guardian dog that was provoked while engaged in herding or guarding
livestock on grazing land;
10 →raises the maximum award from $50,000 to $75,000 in arbitration and at trial for a bodily
11 injury claim resulting from a dog attack, not including certain costs; and
12 →makes technical and conforming changes.
2. Line 51:
51 (A) on the individual's private property; and
52 (B) while the dog is reasonably secured within a fence or other enclosure; and
53 (iii) the animal described in Subsection (3)(a) entered the individual's private
54 property without consent; { or}
55 (b) (i) the injury or death is to a trespasser who is in violation of Subsection
56 76-6-206(2); and
57 (ii) the injury or death occurs:
58 (A) on the individual's private property; and
59 (B) while the dog is reasonably secured within a fence or other enclosure {.} ; or
(c) (i) the dog is a livestock guardian dog, as that term is defined in Section 76-6-111; and
(ii) the injury or death occurs:
(A) on public or private land subject to a valid grazing permit or lease held by the owner of
the dog;
(B) as a result of a provocation of the livestock guardian dog by negligent, reckless, or
intentional conduct; and
(C) while the dog was engaged in guarding or herding livestock.
