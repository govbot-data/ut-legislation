20Joint Resolution Regarding Constitutional Authority2025 SECOND SPECIAL SESSIONSTATE OF UTAHChief Sponsor: Casey SniderSenate Sponsor: Chris H. WilsonLONG TITLEGeneral Description:This resolution reaffirms the constitutional and statutory role of the Legislature in redistricting.Highlighted Provisions:This resolution:reaffirms the constitutional and statutory role of the Legislature in redistricting;condemns actions that ignore the Utah Constitution and Proposition 4;rejects the unconstitutional use of the special interest groups' congressional map; andurges the judicial branch to safeguard the text of the Utah Constitution.Money Appropriated in this Bill:NoneOther Special Clauses:NoneBe it resolved by the Legislature of the state of Utah:WHEREAS, Article VI, Section 1, of the Utah Constitution vests the power to enact laws in the Legislature and the people of the State of Utah;WHEREAS, Article V, Section 1, of the Utah Constitution prohibits any branch of government from exercising the constitutional power of another branch and protects Utahns from government by judicial decree;WHEREAS, Article IX, Section 1, of the Utah Constitution and Article I, Section 4, of the United States Constitution (the Elections Clause) both vest the power of redistricting solely in the Legislature;WHEREAS, Proposition 4, approved narrowly in 2018, created procedural expectations for redistricting but did not and could not strip the Legislature of its constitutional authority to adopt or reject a congressional map;WHEREAS, in 2020, the Legislature worked with the sponsors of Proposition 4 to make necessary statutory changes to ensure that redistricting remained constitutional, transparent, and accountable to voters;WHEREAS, in the first appeal in League of Women Voters of Utah, et al v. Utah State Legislature, et al (litigation), the Utah Supreme Court contrived a novel and unfounded concept of initiated super laws, preventing future legislatures from amending those laws unless a judge agrees that a policy change is necessary, despite no such restriction appearing in the Utah Constitution;WHEREAS, this super law doctrine contradicts over a century of Utah constitutional interpretation and, if unchecked, threatens the foundation of representative democracy by allowing courts to elevate ordinary statutes and personal preferences of unelected judges above the authority of the people's elected legislators;WHEREAS, in the remedial phase of the litigation, the district court disregarded the Proposition 4 requirements that would have provided an independent commission, transparency, and robust legislative and public involvement;WHEREAS, after still providing some level of legislative hearings and public engagement, the Legislature adopted a congressional map on October 6, 2025, that complied with the Proposition 4 redistricting criteria and was validated using objective, scientific, and statistically based standards without the Legislature using any partisan data or political considerations;WHEREAS, although the Lieutenant Governor informed the court that her office needed to receive a final map no later than November 10, 2025, the court: issued its decision rejecting the Legislature's map at 11:41 PM on November 10, well after the Lieutenant Governor's elections office had closed; did not provide operable map files until well after the final deadline; and has yet to provide map files that do not contain serious errors, including obvious errors with district lines cutting through homes;WHEREAS, the court's late-night decision threw out the Legislature's newly-adopted map and unconstitutionally ordered the Lieutenant Governor to use a map drawn by private special interest groups, which never underwent the Proposition 4 process, never received public scrutiny, and never received legislative approval;WHEREAS, the special interest groups' map heavily favors one political party, eliminates electoral competitiveness, and reflects political goals rather than constitutional fidelity or judicial neutrality;WHEREAS, the actions of the court undermine the separation of powers, disregard the Legislature's constitutional role, erode public confidence in the courts, and insert judicial policymaking into an area explicitly reserved to the Legislature by both the Utah Constitution and the United States Constitution;WHEREAS, the court's repeated delays and last-minute rulings manufactured an arbitrary and artificial sense of emergency that has limited meaningful appellate review before the 2026 general election; andWHEREAS, the people of Utah deserve a redistricting process grounded in constitutional text, transparent public input, and democratic accountability, not a process dictated by unelected judges or private special interest groups:NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of the state of Utah condemns the Utah Supreme Court's activist rewriting of the Utah Constitution and its creation of the unfounded doctrine of super laws.BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature condemns the district court's disregard for the Utah Constitution, for Proposition 4 as enacted by the people, and for the separation of powers that protects Utah's democratic institutions.BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature condemns the court's delays and errors that created an arbitrary and artificial emergency, allowing special interest groups to impose a politically-driven map on the people of Utah.BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature condemns the implementation of a map that violates Article IX of the Utah Constitution, Proposition 4, and the Elections Clause of the United States Constitution.BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature rejects the court-ordered, special interest groups' map and any map that does not reflect the will of the people through their elected legislators.BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature urges the judicial branch to safeguard and follow the text of the Utah Constitution.12-8-25 12:16 PM
